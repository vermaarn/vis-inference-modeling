You are given a list of ACE (Atomized, Causal, Explicit) sentences extracted from a student's comment about a news article. Your task is to build a **dependency graph** with **typed edges** over these sentences.

## Task

For each sentence, determine which other sentences it **depends on** and classify each dependency with an **edge type**. A sentence S_b depends on sentence S_a if understanding or justifying S_b requires the information in S_a.

## Edge Types

Assign exactly one of the following edge types to each dependency:

1. **Causal** — A states a cause or mechanism and B states its effect, or B provides a causal explanation for the observation in A. Use when the link is about *why* something happened or *what* something leads to.
   - *"The Lehman Shock happened in 2008" → "The Lehman Shock caused an economic slump"*
   - *"Fossil fuels occupy more than half of energy materials" → "The expenditure of fossil fuels contributes to global warming"*

2. **Elaboration** — B adds detail, specifies, quantifies, generalises, or provides supporting evidence for A. Also covers encoding interpretation (mapping visual features to data meaning) and any zooming-in or zooming-out on information in A.
   - *"Clean energy has grown" → "Wind power has experienced much growth recently"* (general → specific)
   - *"The graph has tiered color blocks" → "Each block symbolises a different fuel"* (visual → meaning)

3. **Conditional** — B states a consequence, prediction, or counterfactual that is contingent on the premise in A. Often marked by "if…then" or modal language ("may", "could").
   - *"The rate of fossil fuels may remain steady" → "If the rate remains steady then global warming will worsen"*
   - *"If global warming worsens" → "the natural environment in Akita could be negatively affected"*

4. **Evaluative** — B is a value judgement, normative claim, or affective reaction about the content of A.
   - *"Fossil fuels occupy more than half" → "This situation is a problem"*
   - *"The smoke damages my community" → "My town needs more clean energy"*

5. **Questioning** — B raises a question, wonder, or expressed uncertainty triggered by the content of A.
   - *"The use of gas has steadily increased" → "I wonder why the use of gas has increased"*
   - *"Electricity generation has increased" → "I wonder how statistics vary from country to country"*

6. **Contrastive** — B introduces a counterpoint, qualification, surprising tension, or exception to A. Often signalled by "however", "despite", "but", or a juxtaposition of conflicting facts.
   - *"Clean energy has grown" → "However, fossil fuels have been consistently higher"*
   - *"New clean-energy ways have proliferated" → "I wonder why gas and coal still increased"* (the proliferation of clean energy contrasts with fossil growth)

7. **Narrative/Referential** — B continues a personal story or temporal sequence from A, performs a discourse act (e.g. headline) that references A, or links to A primarily through anaphoric reference ("this situation", "at that time", "these events").
   - *"When I was a junior high school student, I had an opportunity" → "At that time, I thought about a solution"*
   - *"I live in Akita" → "Akita cedars grow in Akita"*

A sentence may depend on **zero, one, or many** other sentences. Each dependency edge receives exactly one edge type. Sentences can depend on sentences that appear **before or after** them in the list — ordering does not restrict dependency direction.

## Constraints

1. The graph **must be a DAG** (Directed Acyclic Graph): there must be no cycles. If A depends on B, then B must NOT (directly or transitively) depend on A.
2. Every sentence from the input must appear exactly once in the output.
3. Each node's `id` must be a 0-based integer matching the sentence's position in the input list.
4. `depends_on` is an array of objects, each with `id` (integer) and `edge_type` (one of the 7 labels above). May be empty.
5. Use the exact edge type labels: "Causal", "Elaboration", "Conditional", "Evaluative", "Questioning", "Contrastive", "Narrative/Referential".

## Output format

Return a single JSON object with exactly this shape:

```json
{
  "article_id": <article_id from input>,
  "comment_index": <comment_index from input>,
  "dependency_graph": [
    {
      "id": <int>,
      "sentence": "<the sentence text>",
      "depends_on": [
        { "id": <int>, "edge_type": "<one of the 7 labels>" },
        ...
      ]
    },
    ...
  ]
}
```
