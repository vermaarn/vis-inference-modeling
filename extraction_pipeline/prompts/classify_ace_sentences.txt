1. Visual feature detection
   Raw noticing of chart structure or marks.

2. Visual data extraction
   Reading values, trends, comparisons, increases/decreases from the graph.
   Examples: "Wind and solar increased since 2000." "China produces more than India."

3. Encoding interpretation
   Mapping a visual feature to what it means in the chart.
   Examples: "The y-axis is measured in gigawatt hours." "Yellow-orange blocks represent cleaner fuels."

4. Background knowledge
   Stored world knowledge not directly licensed by the chart.
   Examples: "The Lehman Shock happened in 2008." "Fossil fuels contribute to climate change."

6. Explanatory inference
   Causal or abductive linking between observations and background knowledge.
   Examples: "The 2008 dip relates to the financial crisis." "Population may explain the increase."

7. Predictive / counterfactual inference
   Future-oriented or hypothetical reasoning.
   Examples: "If fossil fuels remain steady, warming will worsen." "Clean energy may dominate later."

8. Evaluative / affective judgment
   Normative stance, concern, alarm, hope, moral reaction.
   Examples: "This is a problem." "This increase is alarming." "This data gives me comfort."

9. Information need / curiosity
   Explicit question, uncertainty, missing explanation.
   Examples: every "I wonder..." / "I ask whether..."

10. Meta / paratext
    Commentary about the graph artifact or task rather than the world.
    Examples: "The headline is…" "Ember published the graph." "The graph gives more insight…"
