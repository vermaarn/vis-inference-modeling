CATEGORIES (use the exact label as the comment_tag):

1. Visual observation
   The sentence describes something that is visible in or directly derived from the chart/visualization. This includes:
   - Naming or describing chart elements: chart type, axes, labels, colors, legends, gridlines, marks, shapes
   - Reading specific data values, numbers, or percentages from the chart
   - Comparing data points shown in the chart
   - Describing trends, patterns, or the overall shape of the data as displayed
   - Describing the layout or visual encoding of the chart
   - Any statement whose truth could be verified by looking at the chart alone

   Examples:

2. Non-visual observation
   The sentence describes something that is NOT visible in the chart. This includes:
   - Personal anecdotes, memories, or self-references
   - Background knowledge or facts about the world not shown in the chart
   - Causal explanations for why something in the data happened
   - Predictions or hypothetical/counterfactual statements
   - Emotional reactions, value judgments, or normative stances
   - Questions or expressions of curiosity
   - Meta-commentary about the chart artifact, source, or task
   - Any statement whose truth CANNOT be verified by looking at the chart alone

   Examples:
   - "I live in Akita."
   - "The Lehman Shock happened in 2008."
   - "Traditional gender stereotypes may affect how often a young person reaches out."
   - "If fossil fuels remain steady, warming will worsen."
   - "This trend is concerning."
   - "I wonder why the trend reversed."

Step 4: Could the statement be verified by looking at the chart alone, without any outside knowledge?
